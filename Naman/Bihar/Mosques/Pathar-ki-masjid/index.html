Pathar Ki Masjid (Patna) Dimensions:
  • Built on a raised stone platform
  • Three domes (central dome is larger and more elaborate)
  • Four small minarets at the corners, two tall minarets at the entrance
  • Entire structure made of stone
  • Indo-Islamic, Mughal architectural style
  • Constructed in 1621 CE by Parvez Shah

Pathar Ki Masjid, also known as the Stone Mosque, is one of Patna’s oldest and most revered Islamic monuments. Located on the banks of the holy Ganga River, this historic mosque stands as a remarkable symbol of Mughal-era architecture and the city’s rich cultural heritage.

Historical Significance

Built in 1621 CE by Parvez Shah, the son of Mughal Emperor Jahangir, Pathar Ki Masjid is a testament to the architectural brilliance of the Mughal period. For centuries, it has served as a vibrant center for prayer, community gatherings, and Islamic learning in Bihar.

Architectural Highlights

  • Constructed entirely from finely carved stone, giving the mosque its iconic name.
  • Features a raised stone platform, three domes (with the central dome being the most prominent), and four corner minarets.
  • The entrance is flanked by two tall minarets, welcoming devotees and visitors alike.
  • Exhibits classic Indo-Islamic and Mughal architectural styles, with intricate stonework and elegant arches.

Cultural Importance

Pathar Ki Masjid is not just a place of worship but also a symbol of Patna’s pluralistic heritage. It attracts devotees, tourists, and history enthusiasts from across the country, especially during major Islamic festivals and community events.

Did you know? Pathar Ki Masjid’s riverside location offers a serene atmosphere for prayer and reflection, making it a beloved landmark for locals and visitors alike.
