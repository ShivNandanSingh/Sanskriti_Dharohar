Mahavir Mandir (Patna)

Mahavir Mandir:Main Prasad

    The main prasad of Mahavir Mandir in Patna is the world-famous Naivedyam Laddu. Prepared with pure desi ghee, gram flour, sugar, cashew, raisins, and cardamom, these laddus are offered to Lord Hanuman and then distributed to devotees.
    Naivedyam laddus are known for their divine taste and purity, and are available in various pack sizes. The prasad is so popular that thousands of kilograms are distributed every month, and it has even received FSSAI certification for quality and hygiene.
    Devotees from across the country visit Mahavir Mandir to receive this sacred prasad as a blessing.

Mahavir Mandir(Patna)

Mahavir Mandir in Patna is open daily from 5:00 AM to 10:30 PM for devotees.
Note: Timings may vary slightly on special occasions and during heavy crowds, especially on Tuesdays and Saturdays.

Common Pooja Items at Mahavir Mandir,Patna

  • Naivedyam Laddu: Special prasad offered to Lord Hanuman, made from gram flour, sugar, cashews, raisins, cardamom, saffron, and ghee.
  • Flowers and Garlands: Used for floral decoration and offerings.
  • Incense Sticks (Agarbatti) & Dhoop: For creating a sacred atmosphere.
  • Oil or Ghee Lamps (Diya): For lighting during aarti and rituals.
  • Sindoor (Vermilion): Especially significant for Hanuman worship.
  • Fruits & Sweets: For bhog and offerings.
  • Panchamrit: A sacred mixture of milk, curd, honey, sugar, and ghee for abhishek.
  • Betel Leaves & Nuts: Traditional offering items.
  • Brass Puja Thali and Bell: For organizing and performing rituals.
  • Kalash (Sacred Pot): Used in various pujas and abhisheks.
  • Red Cloth: Often offered to Lord Hanuman.

Devotees can purchase these items at shops near the temple or inside the temple premises. Special pujas like Rudrabhishek, Hanumat Puja, Satyanarayan Puja, and special floral or sindoor offerings are also performed at the temple.
Note: Naivedyam Laddu is the most famous prasad here and can also be ordered online for home delivery.

Major Festivals at Mahavir Mandir(Patna)

  • Ram Navami: The temple’s grandest festival, celebrating Lord Rama’s birth. Mahavir Mandir opens as early as 2:00 AM to accommodate lakhs of devotees. Special pujas, elaborate decorations, and large-scale prasad distribution (notably the famous Naivedyam laddoo) mark this occasion. Crowd management and security are heightened, and the temple remains open for extended hours.
  • Hanuman Jayanti: Celebrated with devotion as the birth anniversary of Lord Hanuman. Special prayers, chanting of Hanuman Chalisa, and vibrant processions are held. The temple attracts huge crowds of devotees seeking blessings for strength and protection.
  • Chaitra Navratri: A nine-day festival dedicated to Goddess Durga, culminating in Ram Navami. Devotees participate in daily prayers and rituals, and the temple is beautifully decorated.
  • New Year’s Day: The temple sees a massive influx of devotees on January 1st, with special arrangements for darshan and prasad.
  • Other Hindu Festivals: Mahavir Mandir also observes other important Hindu festivals with special rituals and increased footfall.

During major festivals, the temple extends its opening hours and provides extra facilities for devotees.

Marriage at Mahavir Mandir(Patna)

Mahavir Mandir in Patna offers the facility for simple and legally recognized Hindu marriages. The temple trust arranges weddings for couples, especially those from needy families, with the following features:

  • Weddings are conducted in a traditional and spiritual manner, following Hindu rituals.
  • The ceremony is simple—no band, dance party, or loudspeakers are allowed.
  • Maximum 50 guests (including both sides) can attend the ceremony.
  • The temple trust provides priests, a simple meal (priti bhoj), and gifts a sari to the bride and a dhoti to the groom.
  • Applicants must provide valid ID, age proof, and other required documents. Written permission from the Mahavir Mandir Trust is necessary.
  • For needy families, the service is free, and preference is given to those without property or in special circumstances.

To book a marriage or for more details, contact the Mahavir Mandir Trust office in Patna.

Education at Mahavir Mandir

Mahavir Mandir Patna runs a Veda Vidyalaya (Vedic School) called Panini Pragya Peetham near Patna Railway Station. The school offers traditional Vedic education, including chanting of the Vedas and Sanskrit grammar, following the Gurukul system.
Admission is open to Hindu students who have passed class 5, with an age limit of around 12–13 years. All students receive free education, food, and accommodation.
The courses include Veda Bhushan (2 years) and Veda Vibhushan (total 7 years), and the school is affiliated with Maharshi Sandipani National Veda Vidya Pratishthan, Ujjain.
For details, parents can contact the temple office or visit the official website.

Mahavir Mandir is one of the most revered Hindu temples dedicated to Lord Hanuman, situated in the heart of Patna, Bihar, near the Patna Junction railway station. Every year, millions of devotees from across India and abroad visit this temple to seek blessings and experience its divine aura.
The temple is not only a spiritual center but also a symbol of faith and resilience for the people of Bihar.

Historical Legacy

The origins of Mahavir Mandir date back to the early 18th century, believed to have been established by Swami Balanand, a saint of the Ramanandi sect, around 1730 CE. The temple gained immense prominence in 1947, when a large influx of Hindu refugees arrived in Patna following the partition of India.
The present grand marble structure was constructed between 1983 and 1985, under the stewardship of Acharya Kishore Kunal and the Mahavir Mandir Trust, making it one of the largest and most prosperous temples in North India.

Architectural Splendor

Spanning over 10,000 square feet, Mahavir Mandir is a three-level marvel, blending traditional Hindu and contemporary architectural styles. The temple's façade is adorned with intricate carvings and mythological motifs, while its towering spire and vibrant flags create an awe-inspiring sight.
The sanctum houses two unique idols of Lord Hanuman, representing protection for the virtuous and destruction of evil. The temple also features shrines dedicated to Lord Rama, Krishna, Shiva, Durga, and other deities, offering a holistic spiritual experience.

Religious and Cultural Significance

Mahavir Mandir is renowned for its miraculous legends, including the self-manifested idol of Sankat Mochan Hanuman and the ever-abundant Naivedyam prasad. The temple is especially vibrant during festivals like Hanuman Jayanti and Rama Navami, when thousands of devotees gather for grand celebrations and rituals.
The temple is open to people of all faiths, reflecting its inclusive spirit and importance in Patna’s cultural identity.

Facilities and Community Service

Managed by the Shri Mahavir Sthan Nyas Samiti, the temple provides a range of facilities for pilgrims, including clean prasad counters, donation services, and resting areas. The trust is also known for its charitable initiatives, supporting education, healthcare, and disaster relief across Bihar.

Famous Foods in Mahavir Mandir,(Patna)

  • Naivedyam Laddu:
    The iconic prasad of Mahavir Mandir, Naivedyam Laddu, is a delicious sweet inspired by Tirupati's laddu. Made from pure besan (gram flour), ghee, sugar, cashew nuts, raisins, and cardamom, these laddus are prepared by skilled South Indian artisans using traditional methods. The aroma and taste of this prasad are unmatched and have become a symbol of devotion for devotees visiting the temple. Naivedyam Laddu is available in various pack sizes and is renowned for its purity and rich flavor.
  • Other Local Delicacies:
    While Naivedyam Laddu is the star attraction, visitors to Mahavir Mandir and Patna also enjoy popular Bihari dishes such as Litti Chokha (roasted wheat balls with spicy mashed vegetables) and Sattu Paratha (flatbread stuffed with roasted gram flour), which are widely available in the city[6].

Don't miss the divine taste of Naivedyam Laddu when you visit Mahavir Mandir, Patna!
