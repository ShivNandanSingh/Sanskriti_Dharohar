Main Prasad of Mithila Shakti Peeth (Darbhanga)

At Mithila Shakti Peeth, devotees are offered traditional sweets such as peda and laddoo, along with seasonal fruits as the main prasad. These offerings are first dedicated to Goddess Uma (Mahadevi) and then distributed among visitors as a sacred blessing.

Major Festivals at Mithila Shakti Peeth (Darbhanga)

  • Navratri (Chaitra & Ashwin): Celebrated twice a year with special prayers, rituals, and temple decorations. Devotees gather in large numbers to worship the goddess during these nine-day festivals.
  • Durga Puja: Marked by vibrant celebrations, cultural performances, and religious processions honoring Goddess Durga.
  • Diwali: The temple is illuminated with lamps and decorated beautifully as devotees celebrate the festival of lights.
  • Mahashivratri: Devotees observe fasts and offer prayers to Lord Shiva; a grand fair is often organized at the temple.
  • Ram Navami: Celebrates the birth of Lord Rama with special rituals and festivities.
  • Chhath Puja: Devotees worship the Sun God and Chhathi Maiya with great devotion.

Marriage at Mithila Shakti Peeth (Darbhanga)

A marriage solemnized at the holy Mithila Shakti Peeth in Darbhanga is believed to be blessed by Goddess Mahashakti. The temple, one of the 51 sacred Shakti Peethas, is renowned for granting marital bliss and lifelong harmony to couples. Many devotees choose this divine place for their wedding ceremonies to seek the eternal blessings of the Goddess for a happy and prosperous married life.

Education at Mithila Shakti Peeth (Darbhanga)

  • Mithila, the region where the Shakti Peeth is located, has been a historical center of learning, with ancient ashrams of Vedic sages like Gautama, Viswamitra, and Yajnavalkya who contributed to Vedic literature.
  • During the era of the Darbhanga Maharajas, education was modernized. Maharaja Lakshmeshwar Singh upgraded the Raj Anglo Vernacular School to matriculation level and made it a free institution, while also establishing several free middle schools across the estate.
  • Efforts were made to promote Sanskrit, vernacular, English, and female education in the region, reflecting a blend of traditional and modern educational values.
  • Today, Darbhanga remains an educational hub in Mithila, with institutions inspired by this legacy of learning and cultural advancement.

Food at Mithila Shakti Peeth (Darbhanga)

At Mithila Shakti Peeth, devotees are offered traditional vegetarian prasad during religious festivals and special occasions. Alcohol and non-vegetarian food are not permitted inside the temple.

  • Prasad: Sweets, fruits, and Mithila snacks
  • Special Occasions: Chhappan Bhog (56 Bhog) – a grand feast of 56 vegetarian dishes
  • Popular Dishes: Tarua (vegetable fritters), Tilauri (sesame sweets), Kachari (crispy snacks)

All offerings are prepared with devotion and distributed to devotees as a symbol of blessings from the goddess.

Mithila Shakti Peeth is one of the most revered Shakti Peethas, located near Janakpur station in Darbhanga, Bihar, close to the India-Nepal border. This sacred temple is dedicated to Goddess Durga, worshipped here as Uma or Mahadevi, and holds immense significance in Hindu mythology as the site where the left shoulder of Goddess Sati is believed to have fallen.

Historical & Mythological Significance

  • According to legend, after Goddess Sati's self-immolation, Lord Shiva wandered the universe in grief, carrying her body. Lord Vishnu, to restore cosmic order, used his Sudarshan Chakra to dismember Sati's body, and her left shoulder fell at Mithila, making it a powerful center of divine feminine energy.
  • The temple is not only a spiritual hub but also closely connected to the region's history, as Mithila is celebrated as the birthplace of Sita, consort of Lord Rama, adding to its cultural and religious significance.

Architectural Features

  • The temple is built in a fort-like style with a pristine white exterior and four minar-type towers that enhance its grandeur.
  • A towering entrance gate, adorned with intricate sculptural work and a tall gopuram, welcomes devotees and visitors, symbolizing the temple's magnificence.
  • The temple complex features spacious courtyards for gatherings, subsidiary shrines dedicated to other deities, and beautiful murals and carvings depicting scenes from Hindu mythology.
  • In front of the temple, a colorful fountain adds to the visual appeal and serves as a popular spot for visitors.
  • The temple is situated on a raised platform, accessible by a short flight of stairs, and houses a sacred pond believed to have healing properties.

Rituals, Festivals & Timings

  • The temple follows traditional Hindu rituals, including morning and evening aarti, abhishekam (ritual bathing of the deity), and bhog offering (food offerings distributed as prasad).
  • Major festivals celebrated here include Navratri (twice a year), Ram Navami, Janaki Navami, Durga Puja, Shivratri, and Diwali. During these times, the temple is beautifully decorated with flowers and lamps, and special ceremonies are held, attracting devotees from across India and abroad.
  • Temple Timings: Open daily from 6:00 AM to 8:00 PM.

How to Reach

  • By Road: Well-connected via National Highway 27 from Patna (225 km, approx. 6 hours). Buses and taxis are available from major cities.
  • By Rail: The nearest railway station is Janakpur, with connections to Patna, Kolkata, and Delhi.
  • By Air: The closest airport is Biratnagar Airport in Nepal (170 km away), with taxis and buses available for onward travel.
