Main Prasad of Ajgaibinath Temple (Sultanganj)

The main prasad offered at Ajgaivinath Temple is belpatra (bael leaves), milk, and holy water from the Ganges. Devotees also offer fruits and sweets to Lord Shiva. After the rituals, these offerings are distributed as prasad to the devotees.

Key Features of Ajgaibinath Temple (Sultanganj)

  • Location: Situated on a rock island in the Ganges River, Sultanganj, Bhagalpur, Bihar
  • Access: Reachable by boat from Murli Hills and via a walk bridge (especially during monsoon)

  • Timings: 6:00 AM – 8:00 PM (may vary on festivals)

Major Festivals at Ajgaibinath Temple (Sultanganj)

  • Shravani Mela: Month-long festival (July–August), when lakhs of devotees collect holy Ganga water and begin their Kanwar Yatra to Baidyanath Dham. This is the most significant and grand celebration at the temple.
  • Mahashivratri: Celebrated with great devotion to Lord Shiva, with special prayers and rituals.
  • Ram Navami: Marks the birth of Lord Rama, observed with religious ceremonies.
  • Vasant Panchami: Celebrated in spring, dedicated to Saraswati and marking the change of season.
  • Bhadra Purnima: Full moon festival observed with rituals.
  • Ganga Dussehra: Celebrates the descent of the holy river Ganga, with special offerings and rituals at the temple.

These festivals attract thousands of pilgrims and are celebrated with devotion and grandeur at Ajgaivinath Temple every year.

Marriage at Ajgaibinath Temple (Sultanganj)

Ajgaivinath Temple in Sultanganj, Bihar, is a sacred and ancient Shiva temple located on a rock island in the holy Ganges River. Marrying here is considered highly auspicious, as the temple is believed to be self-manifested and continuously blessed by the sacred river. The spiritual atmosphere and the presence of Lord Shiva make the marriage ceremony especially divine and memorable for couples seeking blessings for a harmonious life together[1][2][5].

  • Location: Sultanganj, Bhagalpur district, Bihar
  • Deity: Lord Shiva (Ajgaivinath)
  • Special Significance: Temple built on a rock in the Ganga, famous for uninterrupted worship and holy rituals
  • Marriage here: Considered sacred, with blessings of Lord Shiva and the Ganges for a prosperous married life

Note: For marriage arrangements, consult temple authorities in advance as the site is a major pilgrimage destination, especially during festivals and the Shravani Mela.

Food at Ajgaibinath Temple (Sultanganj)

At Ajgaivinath Temple, food is generally not provided as a regular service inside the temple premises. However, during major festivals like Shravani Mela and Mahashivratri, devotees may receive prasad (holy offering) such as sweets and fruits.
For full meals or snacks, visitors can find several restaurants and food stalls near the temple area, offering popular local dishes like litti chokha, kachori, samosa, and sweets.
Please note: There are no large-scale free food distribution (langar) services at Ajgaivinath Temple.

  • Prasad: Sweets, fruits (during festivals)
  • Nearby food: Litti chokha, kachori, samosa, sweets, and more
  • No regular free meal service inside the temple

Ajgaivinath Temple (also known as Ajgaibinath Dham or Gaibinath Mahadev) is an ancient and revered Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Shiva, located in Sultanganj, Bhagalpur district, Bihar. Uniquely perched on a rock island in the middle of the holy river Ganges, the temple is a major pilgrimage destination, especially during the annual Shravani Mela. The temple is believed to be Swayambhu (self-manifested), and its sanctity is enhanced by the northward flow of the Ganges at this location, considered highly auspicious by devotees.

History & Legends

According to ancient legends, Lord Shiva received his divine bow, Ajgav, here, giving the temple its name. The site is also associated with Sage Jahnu, whose ashram was a center of learning and meditation. The river Ganga, disturbed by her currents, was swallowed by Sage Jahnu and later released, earning her the name Jahnavi.
  • The temple's origins are traced to the Treta Yuga, and it is believed Lord Rama worshipped here before his battle with Ravana[1].
  • Architecturally, the temple features remarkable rock-carved sculptures and ancient inscriptions, some dating back to the Pala period. The area has also yielded relics from the Gupta and Maurya eras, including coins, seals, terracotta, and Buddhist images[2][3][7].
  • The temple has withstood invasions, notably surviving attempts at destruction by the invader Kala Pahar.

Religious Significance

Ajgaivinath Temple is one of the holiest Shiva temples in Bihar and Jharkhand, alongside Basukinath and Baidyanath temples. Its unique location on the Ganges and the continuous worship traditions make it a focal point for devotees, especially during the Shravani Mela, when millions of pilgrims collect holy water from here to offer at Baidyanath Dham in Deoghar.

Festivals & Celebrations

Major festivals celebrated:
  • Mahashivratri
  • Ram Navami
  • Vasant Panchami
  • Bhadra Purnima
  • Ganga Dussehra
  • Shravani Mela (month-long, July-August): The most significant festival, drawing lakhs of devotees who begin their Kanwar Yatra from Ajgaivinath to Baidyanath Dham.

Architectural Highlights

  • Temple is built on a solid rock protruding from the Ganga, accessible only by boat from Murli Hills, Sultanganj.
  • Features exquisite rock panel sculptures and inscriptions, some among the finest in India, mostly from the later Pala period.
  • During monsoon, the swirling waters of the Ganga wash the temple's base, creating a mesmerizing sight.
