Raj Rajeshwari Devi Temple(Muzaffarpur)

Main Prasad of Raj Rajeshwari Devi Temple(Muzaffarpur)

  • Sindoor (Vermilion) and Doob (Sacred Grass):
    The main prasad offered and distributed at Raj Rajeshwari Devi Temple is sindoor and doob (a type of sacred grass). Devotees believe that offering these to the goddess pleases her, and receiving them as prasad brings blessings and wish fulfillment.
    Costly or elaborate offerings are not permitted; the temple tradition emphasizes simplicity and devotion.

Devotees receive sindoor and doob as the primary prasad after worship at this temple.

The temple is open daily from 4:00 AM to 11:00 PM for devotees.

Raj Rajeshwari Devi Temple & Ritual Schedule

  • Temple Opening Hours: 4:00 AM – 11:00 PM (every day)
  • Morning Pooja & Aarti: 6:00 AM (Aarti at 6:30 AM)
  • Evening Pooja & Aarti: 4:30 PM – 10:00 PM (Aarti at 9:00 PM)

Special aartis and rituals are held during Navratri and other festivals.

Common Pooja Items at Raj Rajeshwari Devi Temple

  • Fresh flowers (especially red or yellow)
  • Coconut
  • Fruits (banana, apple, etc.)
  • Incense sticks (agarbatti)
  • Camphor (kapoor)
  • Diya (oil lamp)
  • Kumkum and sindoor
  • Rice (akshat)
  • Betel leaves and nuts (paan, supari)
  • Sweets (like peda or laddu)
  • Cloth (chunari or saree for the goddess)
  • Dakshina (offering money)

These items are commonly used by devotees for worship and offerings at Raj Rajeshwari Devi Temple, Muzaffarpur.

Major Festivals at Raj Rajeshwari Devi Temple

  • Chaitra Navratri: Celebrated in spring, this nine-day festival sees thousands of devotees visiting the temple for special aartis and rituals.
  • Sharadiya (Durga) Navratri: The most prominent festival at the temple, held in autumn. The temple is packed with devotees, and grand morning and evening aartis are performed daily.
  • Durga Puja: Marked by vibrant celebrations, decorations, and special prayers to Goddess Durga.
  • Diwali: The temple is beautifully illuminated, and special pujas are performed to celebrate the festival of lights.

Navratri and Durga Puja are the most important festivals here, drawing large crowds and creating a spiritually charged atmosphere throughout the temple.

Education at Raj Rajeshwari Devi Temple

Raj Rajeshwari Devi Temple is not only a spiritual center but also supports community development through educational and social activities. The temple trust organizes cultural programs, spiritual discourses, and promotes awareness about traditional knowledge and values.
While the temple itself does not run formal schools, it encourages learning and hosts events that foster education and social harmony among devotees and local residents.

Raj Rajeshwari Devi Temple, popularly known as Devi Mandir, stands as a radiant center of faith and spirituality in the heart of Muzaffarpur, Bihar. Located on Ramna Road, this revered temple is dedicated to Goddess Durga in her powerful Shodashi Tripura Sundari form, worshipped as the supreme embodiment of power, compassion, and wisdom.

Established on 28 June 1941 by Umashankar Prasad (Bachcha Babu) after his heartfelt wish was fulfilled, the temple has become a sanctuary for devotees seeking blessings, protection, and the fulfillment of desires. The golden idol of Maa Raj Rajeshwari, adorned with sindoor and dub (sacred grass), is believed to grant every sincere prayer, making the temple a magnet for pilgrims from far and wide.

The temple’s atmosphere is especially electrifying during Chaitra and Sharadiya Navratri, when thousands gather for grand aartis, lighting lamps, and performing traditional rituals like khoycha bharna (offering to the goddess) and mannat mangna (making a wish). From saptami to navami, the temple resonates with devotion, and it is widely believed that Maa Shodashi fulfills the wishes of all who come with true faith.

Architecturally, the temple is a blend of tradition and serenity, offering a peaceful retreat amidst the bustle of the city. Its sanctum, vibrant with devotional energy, is open to devotees year-round, with the most vibrant celebrations during Navratri and other major Hindu festivals.

Raj Rajeshwari Devi Temple is not just a place of worship, but a living legacy of miracles, unwavering faith, and divine grace—a must-visit for anyone seeking spiritual solace in Muzaffarpur.
