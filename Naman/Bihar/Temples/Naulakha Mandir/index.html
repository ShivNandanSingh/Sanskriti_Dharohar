Main Prasad of Naulakha Mandir(Begusarai)

The main prasad offered at Naulakha Mandir, Begusarai, is traditional sweets and fruits that devotees present to the deities, especially during major festivals and religious occasions. On auspicious days like Maha Shivratri and Purnima, special prasad such as laddus, peda, and seasonal fruits are prepared and distributed among devotees after the rituals.
The prasad reflects the simple and pure traditions of Mithila culture, emphasizing devotion and community sharing.
Receiving prasad at Naulakha Mandir is considered highly auspicious and is believed to bring blessings and good fortune to devotees.

Common Pooja Items at Naulakha Mandir (Begusarai)

  • Flowers: Fresh marigold, rose, and lotus flowers for offering to the deities.
  • Incense Sticks (Agarbatti): Used for creating a fragrant and devotional atmosphere.
  • Diya (Oil Lamp): Clay or brass lamps filled with ghee or oil for aarti rituals.
  • Kumkum & Haldi: Red vermillion and turmeric powder for tilak and offerings.
  • Rice (Akshat): Unbroken rice grains used in various rituals.
  • Fruits & Sweets: Seasonal fruits and traditional sweets as prasad for the deities.
  • Coconut: Offered during special pujas and rituals.
  • Bel Patra & Bael Leaves: Especially for Lord Shiva, commonly worshipped in the temple.
  • Milk, Honey, and Curd: Used for abhishek (ritual bathing of idols), especially during Maha Shivratri.
  • Cloth (Vastra): New clothes for adorning the idols, especially during festivals.
  • Kalash (Sacred Pot): Filled with water, mango leaves, and coconut for auspicious rituals.
  • Camphor (Kapoor): Used in aarti and for purifying the environment.
  • Betel Leaves & Nuts: Traditional offering in Hindu rituals.
  • Holy Ganga Water: Used for ritual purification and abhishek, especially during fairs and festivals.

All these items are available at local puja item shops near Naulakha Mandir and in Begusarai city.

Naulakha Mandir is a magnificent Hindu temple located in Bishanpur, Begusarai district, Bihar. The temple is renowned for its stunning architecture, intricate carvings, and historical significance. Built in 1953 by the revered saint Mahavir Das, the temple derives its name from the construction cost of nine lakh rupees, a huge sum at the time, which is reflected in the name "Naulakha" (meaning "Nine Lakhs")[1][2][3][4][5].

Architectural Beauty

The temple's construction took nearly 10 years to complete, resulting in a structure that captivates visitors with its grandeur. The walls are adorned with exquisite artwork and carvings, while the floor and statues are crafted from white marble brought from Makrana, Rajasthan. The ceilings feature beautiful glasswork sourced from Kolkata, making the temple's interiors shine with a unique brilliance. The temple houses idols of Lord Rama, Sita, Lakshman, and several other Hindu deities, all decorated with fine clothes, shiny gems, and ornate headgear[3][4].

Spiritual Significance

Naulakha Mandir is a major religious and cultural center in Begusarai. Devotees come here to seek blessings, pray for good fortune, and perform rituals for long life and success. The temple is especially crowded during festivals and New Year celebrations, attracting visitors from across the region. The temple complex also features a serene pond and a landscaped park, which has become a popular selfie point for young visitors[3][4].

Unique Features

  • Historical Construction: Built over 10 years at a cost of nine lakh rupees in 1953.
  • Marble & Glasswork: Marble from Rajasthan and glass from Kolkata were used for its intricate design.
  • Divine Statues: Idols of Lord Rama, Sita, Lakshman, and other deities in white marble.
  • Artistic Carvings: Outstanding carvings and artwork on the walls and ceilings.
  • Serene Surroundings: Includes a beautiful pond and a park, ideal for relaxation and photography.

Famous Food Near Naulakha Mandir,Begusarai

  • Litti Chokha: The most iconic Bihari dish, made of roasted wheat balls stuffed with spiced sattu (roasted gram flour) and served with mashed vegetables like eggplant, tomato, and potato.
  • Sattu Paratha: Flatbread stuffed with seasoned roasted gram flour, a staple breakfast and snack in Begusarai.
  • Pantua (Atom Bomb): A sweet similar to gulab jamun, but larger and known locally as "Atom Bomb" for its size and rich taste.
  • Malpua: A syrup-soaked sweet pancake, especially popular during festivals.
  • Street Snacks: Samosa, kachori, pakora, chaat, and golgappa are widely available at local stalls.

Don’t miss these traditional Bihari flavors when you visit Naulakha Mandir and Begusarai!
