Main Prasad of Sundarnath Temple (Araria)

The main prasad offered at Sundarnath Temple typically includes panchamrit (a mixture of milk, honey, curd, ghee, and sugar) and seasonal fruits.
Devotees receive this prasad after performing rituals and worship of Lord Shiva.

Major Festivals at Sundarnath Temple (Araria)

  • Maha Shivaratri: The biggest festival at Sundarnath Temple, celebrated with great devotion. Festivities and a large fair last for 15–20 days, attracting thousands of devotees from India and Nepal.
    Special: The Maldhari fair for business and cultural exchange also takes place during this time.
  • Purnima (Full Moon): Every full moon, thousands visit the temple to offer prayers and perform rituals.
  • Makkar Mela: Begins on the first Sunday after Makar Sankranti and continues for four to five Sundays. It is a vibrant gathering, especially for the Maldhari community, featuring trade and cultural activities.

During festivals, the temple premises are filled with devotional music, rituals, and a fair-like atmosphere. Nepali devotees often use their currency for offerings and purchases during the fair.

Food at Sundarnath Temple (Araria)

Sundarnath Temple, located in Araria, Bihar, is not only a center of spiritual devotion but also a place where devotees are offered simple and traditional food as part of the temple’s hospitality.

  • Prasad: Bilva leaves, fresh milk, honey, curd, and water are offered to the Shiva Lingam and later distributed as prasad to devotees.
  • Community Meals (Bhandara): During major festivals like Maha Shivaratri and Purnima, the temple organizes free community meals for devotees, serving traditional vegetarian dishes such as rice, dal, sabzi, and sweets.

Welcome to Sundarnath Temple, a sacred and historical shrine dedicated to Lord Shiva, located in the serene village of Kursakanta, Araria, near the India–Nepal border. Revered by devotees from both India and Nepal, this temple is a vibrant center of faith, history, and cultural unity.
Sundarnath Dham is not just a place of worship—it is a living legend where mythology, devotion, and community spirit come together.

Historical Significance

The origins of Sundarnath Temple are deeply rooted in the Mahabharata era. According to legend, the Pandavas, during their exile (Agyatvas), worshipped Lord Shiva here and performed jalabhishek (ritual bathing of the Shiva Lingam) to seek strength and protection. It is believed they hid their divine weapons under a tree in front of the temple before proceeding to King Virata’s court.

Legend: “It is said that the Pandavas, along with their mother Kunti, stayed in this region and offered prayers to Lord Shiva at Sundarnath Temple. The vibrations of their ancient prayers are still felt by devotees today.”

Architectural Beauty

The temple is built in the classic North Indian Nagara style, featuring a tall shikara (spire) and a spacious mandap (hall) for gatherings. In front of the temple lies a sacred pond (koneru), where devotees perform ritual baths before offering water to the Shiva Lingam. The minimalist yet majestic design creates an atmosphere of peace and spiritual focus.

Religious Importance

  • Devotees believe that sincere prayers at Sundarnath Temple are always heard by Lord Shiva, bringing blessings and fulfillment of wishes.
  • The temple is a hub for community events, cultural exchange, and spiritual learning, uniting people across borders.
  • Special poojas include offerings of Bilva leaves, milk, water, honey, and curd to the Shiva Lingam, along with lighting incense and diyas.

Festivals and Celebrations

Maha Shivaratri: The grandest festival here, celebrated with vibrant fairs, devotional music, and rituals lasting up to 20 days. Thousands of devotees from India and Nepal participate.
Purnima: On every full moon, the temple sees a surge of devotees offering prayers and singing hymns.
Makkar Mela: Starting from the first Sunday after Makar Sankranti, this fair continues for several weeks, bringing together the Maldhari community for trade and cultural festivities. Nepalese currency is accepted during the fair, highlighting the cross-border harmony.

Unique Features & Facilities

  • Guest House: Accommodation for pilgrims and visitors from distant places.
  • Security: Modern CCTV surveillance ensures safety for all visitors.
  • Multi-purpose Venue: The temple hosts rituals like upnayan, mundan, and weddings, making it a center for life’s important ceremonies.
  • Accessibility: Well-connected by road and rail, with nearby bus stands and railway stations. The best time to visit is between October and December.

Mystical Beliefs

Many believe that the Shiva Lingam here has mystical powers, with stories of divine lights on new moon nights and sightings of sacred serpents entwined around the Lingam. The temple’s aura is said to fulfill countless wishes, drawing devotees back again and again.
