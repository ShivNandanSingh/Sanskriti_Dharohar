Main Altar of Padri Ki Haveli (Patna)

The main altar of Padri Ki Haveli stands as the spiritual heart of the church, adorned with elegant colonial-era design and intricate carvings. It serves as the focal point for prayer and mass, reflecting the church’s rich heritage and devotion.
The altar is framed by tall white columns and features Christian iconography, offering a serene space for worship and contemplation.

Major Festivals at Padri Ki Haveli (Patna)

  • Christmas (25th December): The most significant festival, celebrated with midnight mass, special prayers, carol singing, nativity plays, and elaborate decorations. Festivities begin in the week leading up to Christmas, including community visits and charitable activities.
  • New Year: Special prayers and gatherings are held to mark the beginning of the new year, drawing many visitors to the church during the holiday season.

Padri Ki Haveli, also known as St. Mary's Church, stands as a magnificent symbol of faith, history, and architectural splendor in Patna. Established in 1713, it is the oldest church in Bihar and a beacon of peace and spiritual heritage for generations of believers and visitors alike.

Historical Legacy

Nestled in the heart of Patna City, Padri Ki Haveli was originally built by Roman Catholic missionaries and has witnessed over three centuries of history. The church has played a pivotal role in the spiritual and social fabric of the region, serving as a center for worship, charity, and community gatherings.

  • Founded in 1713 by Father Joseph Tieffenthaler.
  • Reconstructed in 1772 after destruction during the British invasion.
  • Mother Teresa visited and served here during her early years in India.

Architectural Marvel

Padri Ki Haveli is renowned for its stunning blend of European and Mughal architectural styles. The church features grand arches, high ceilings, and intricate woodwork, creating an atmosphere of tranquility and reverence.

Spiritual Significance & Community Life

The church is not only a place of worship but also a vibrant center for social service, education, and interfaith harmony. Regular masses, prayer meetings, and festive celebrations like Christmas and Easter bring together people from all walks of life.

  • Daily and Sunday Mass services in Hindi and English.
  • Community outreach programs and charity work.
  • Special events during major Christian festivals.

Visitor Information

Padri Ki Haveli welcomes visitors of all faiths to experience its serene ambiance and rich history. The church is open throughout the week, and guided tours are available on request.
