Major Festivals at Har Ki Pauri

Har Ki Pauri Festivals (Haridwar)

Har Ki Pauri is the most sacred ghat in Haridwar, on the banks of the Ganga River. It is famous for its grand festivals and daily rituals that attract millions of devotees and tourists every year.

Daily Ganga Aarti

  • Performed every morning and evening at Brahmakund ghat.
  • Priests wave large fire lamps, chant hymns, and devotees float diyas (lamps) on the river.
  • The ceremony is a mesmerizing sight with bells, chants, and glowing lamps reflecting on the Ganga.
    Devotees believe attending the aarti brings blessings and spiritual peace.

Kumbh Mela

  • Held every 12 years; one of the largest religious gatherings in the world.
  • Millions gather at Har Ki Pauri to take a holy dip in the Ganges, believed to cleanse sins and grant moksha (liberation).
  • Next Haridwar Kumbh Mela: 2033.

Ardh Kumbh Mela

  • Celebrated every 6 years, midway between two Kumbh Melas.
  • Draws huge crowds for ritual bathing and religious ceremonies at Har Ki Pauri.

Vaisakhi

  • Annual harvest festival in April, important for both Hindus and Sikhs.
  • Pilgrims gather at Har Ki Pauri for ritual bathing and prayers.

Kanwar Yatra

  • Occurs during the month of Shravan (July–August).
  • Thousands of Shiva devotees (Kanwariyas) collect holy Ganga water to offer at Shiva temples across India.

Somvati Amavasya

  • Observed on new moon days that fall on a Monday.
  • Considered highly auspicious for taking a dip in the Ganga at Har Ki Pauri.

Marriage at Har Ki Pauri

Har Ki Pauri in Haridwar is a sacred ghat on the banks of the Ganga River, renowned for spiritual rituals and Hindu weddings. Couples seeking blessings often perform traditional marriage ceremonies here, accompanied by Vedic chants and holy fire rituals.

  • Location: Har Ki Pauri, Haridwar, Uttarakhand

  • Note: Prior permission from temple authorities is required for organizing ceremonies.

About Har Ki Pauri

Har Ki Pauri is the most revered and iconic ghat on the banks of the holy river Ganga in Haridwar, Uttarakhand. This ancient site, whose name means "Steps of Lord Shiva," is believed to be the exact spot where the Ganga leaves the mountains and enters the plains. It is a focal point for millions of Hindu pilgrims who come to take a holy dip and wash away their sins.

Historical Significance

Established by King Vikramaditya in the 1st century BC in memory of his brother Bharthari, Har Ki Pauri has been expanded over centuries. The ghat was extended to its present form in 1819, and again in 1938 and 1986, now stretching 100 feet with 60 broad steps leading down to the river.

Spiritual Experience

  • Ganga Aarti: The mesmerizing evening Ganga Aarti is a spectacle of light, sound, and devotion. Hundreds of lamps are floated on the river as priests chant hymns, creating a magical and spiritual atmosphere.
  • Holy Bath: Devotees believe that bathing at Har Ki Pauri cleanses the soul and paves the way for salvation.
  • Festivals: During Kumbh Mela and other festivals, the ghat becomes a sea of faith, drawing pilgrims from across the world.

Visitor Information

Har Ki Pauri is open throughout the year and is easily accessible from Haridwar railway station. The best time to visit is during the evening aarti, when the ghat glows with thousands of lamps and the air resonates with devotional chants.
