Note: Prasad offerings may vary based on local traditions and festivals.

Kanchani Tal Opening Timings

Open: Sunrise to Sunset
Note: The best time to visit is from May to October as the trail is closed during heavy snowfall in winter.

Kanchani Tal Timings & Ritual Schedule

  • Morning Pooja: 6:00 AM – 8:00 AM
  • Evening Pooja: 5:00 PM – 6:30 PM

Note: Timings may vary during festivals and special occasions.

Common Pooja Items at Kanchani Tal

  • Incense sticks (Agarbatti)
  • Camphor (Kapoor)
  • Flowers (Fresh or dried)
  • Rice grains (Akshat)
  • Red/yellow cloth
  • Fruits and sweets (Prasad)
  • Diya (Oil lamp)
  • Kumkum and Haldi (Vermilion & Turmeric)
  • Holy water (Gangajal)
  • Coconut

Key Features of Kanchani Tal

  • Trekking: Only accessible by trekking (approx. 16 km from Madhyamaheshwar)
  • Camping: Camping allowed near the lake; ideal for overnight stays
  • Natural Beauty: Surrounded by Himalayan peaks, meadows, and wildflowers
  • Spiritual Ambience: Peaceful environment suitable for meditation and introspection
  • Luggage Facility: Extra luggage can be kept at the base village free of cost
  • No Permanent Structures: No hotels, shops, or restaurants at the lake
  • Nearest Railway: Rishikesh
  • Nearest Airport: Jolly Grant, Dehradun

Note: All amenities are basic and nature-based; visitors should carry their own supplies.

Major Festivals at Kanchani Tal

  • Local Fairs: Small fairs and gatherings are held by villagers and trekkers, celebrating the natural beauty and spiritual atmosphere of the lake.
  • Religious Pooja: Devotees occasionally perform special poojas and rituals at the lake, especially during auspicious Hindu festivals.
  • Trekking Season: The area sees increased activity during the summer trekking season, coinciding with various regional festivals.

Note: There are no large-scale or nationally recognized festivals specific to Kanchani Tal.

Education at Kanchani Tal

Currently, there is no formal educational institution or program provided at Kanchani Tal. The site is primarily known for its natural beauty and religious significance, attracting trekkers and pilgrims rather than students or academic groups.

Food at Kanchani Tal

At Kanchani Tal, food is not directly provided by a temple. During treks, vegetarian meals such as breakfast, packed lunch, and dinner are arranged by trekking organizers or camps. Non-vegetarian meals are not served. It is recommended to carry your own snacks and energy foods for the journey.

Introduction

Kanchani Tal is a mesmerizing high-altitude glacial lake nestled in the Garhwal Himalayas, near the sacred Kedarnath region of Uttarakhand. Renowned for its crystal-clear waters and tranquil surroundings, Kanchani Tal is a paradise for trekkers, nature enthusiasts, and spiritual seekers.

Natural Beauty

Surrounded by snow-clad peaks and lush alpine meadows, Kanchani Tal offers breathtaking panoramic views of the mighty Himalayas. The lake's pristine blue waters reflect the towering mountains and the ever-changing sky, creating a magical and serene atmosphere. During the summer months, the meadows around the lake bloom with vibrant wildflowers, adding to its enchanting charm.

Did You Know?
"Kanchani" means "golden," and the lake is said to shimmer with a golden hue during sunrise and sunset, captivating all who visit.

Adventure and Trekking

Reaching Kanchani Tal is an adventure in itself. The trek passes through dense forests, gushing streams, and scenic ridges, offering an immersive Himalayan experience. The journey is challenging but rewarding, making it a favorite among seasoned trekkers and explorers.

Key Features

  • Altitude: Around 4,200 meters above sea level
  • Dimensions: Approximately 120 meters long and 80 meters wide
  • Location: Near Kedarnath, Uttarakhand
  • Best Time to Visit: May to October, when the trails are open and the landscape is lush and vibrant

Why Visit?

Whether you seek solitude, adventure, or spiritual rejuvenation, Kanchani Tal promises an unforgettable experience. The untouched beauty, peaceful ambiance, and awe-inspiring vistas make it a must-visit destination for anyone exploring the Himalayas of Uttarakhand.

Famous Food Near Kanchani Tal

  • Aloo Ke Gutke: Spiced boiled potatoes roasted with local herbs.
  • Kafuli: Rich curry made from spinach and fenugreek leaves.
  • Bhatt ki Churkani: Creamy black soybean dish, best with rice.
  • Chainsoo: Smoky, protein-rich gravy from roasted black lentils.
  • Phaanu: Lentil delicacy cooked with regional spices.
  • Gulgula: Sweet, deep-fried wheat flour balls.
  • Kandalee ka Saag: Wild leafy greens unique to the region.

Most restaurants near the temple serve North and South Indian vegetarian food, along with local Garhwali and Kumaoni specialties.
