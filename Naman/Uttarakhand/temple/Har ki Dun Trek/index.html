Har ki Dun Trek

Main Prasad of Har ki Dun Trek

During the Har ki Dun Trek, the main prasad offered at the ancient temples (like Someshwar Mahadev Temple in Osla) is usually jaggery (gur) and rice. Sometimes, local sweets or fruits are also distributed among devotees and trekkers as blessings.

Har ki Dun Trek Opening Timings

  • Spring/Summer: April to June
  • Autumn: September to November
Closed during monsoon (July-August) and heavy winter (December-March) for most trekkers.

Har ki Dun Trek Timings & Ritual Schedule

Daily Pooja: 6:30 AM – 7:00 AM

Pooja is performed at sunrise at various campsites or village temples along the trek.

Common Pooja Items at Har ki Dun Trek

  • Small Puja Thali (plate)
  • Incense sticks (Agarbatti)
  • Camphor (Kapoor)
  • Matchbox or lighter
  • Small diya (oil lamp) or tea-light candle
  • Roli/Kumkum (vermilion)
  • Rice grains (Akshat)
  • Flowers (fresh or dried)
  • Chandan (sandalwood paste) – optional
  • Prayer beads (Rudraksha mala) – optional
  • Small container of water (for offering)
  • Prasad (sweets or dry fruits)

Key Features of Har ki Dun Trek

  • Accommodation: Tented camps (2-3 people per tent), guesthouses, or village huts along the route[2][5][9].
  • Meals: All meals included during the trek; simple, nutritious food provided by trek support staff[2][5].
  • Toilets: Toilet tents available at campsites[2].
  • Guides & Support: Experienced mountain guides, helpers, and support staff (qualified in first aid and mountaineering)[2][5].
  • Trekking Equipment: Sleeping bags, camping mattresses, camping stools, mess tent, safety equipment[2].
  • Transport: Shared vehicle transport from Dehradun to trek start point and back[2].
  • Permits: All forest permits and fees included[2].
  • First Aid: First aid kit and basic medical support available[2][5].
  • Water: Water provided at campsites; trekkers advised to carry personal water bottles[2].
  • Other Amenities: Cleanliness maintained, peaceful environment, friendly staff, value for money[5].

Major Festivals at Har ki Dun Trek

Osla Village Mela
  • Held during monsoon (July-August)
  • Lasts about two weeks
  • Includes local fairs, folk dances, and rituals at the ancient Someshwar (Lord Shiva) temple
Temple Celebrations
  • Special pujas and gatherings at the Duryodhana temple in Osla
  • Festive atmosphere with cultural performances

These festivals offer trekkers a chance to witness the unique culture and traditions of the Har ki Dun valley villages during their journey.
Tip: Plan your trek in monsoon if you wish to experience the vibrant local festivals.

Food at Har ki Dun Trek

  • The temple offers simple vegetarian food to devotees, especially during festivals and special occasions.
  • Prasad (holy offering) typically includes sweets, fruits, and sometimes cooked rice or khichdi.
  • Nearby eateries and restaurants serve North Indian, South Indian, and traditional Garhwali dishes such as Aloo ke Gutke and Phaanu.

Har ki Dun Trek Dimensions:
  • Total Trek Distance: 44–57 km (varies by route)
  • Maximum Altitude: 3,566 meters / 11,700 feet (Har Ki Dun Valley)
  • Base Camp: Sankri (1,950 meters / 6,400 feet approx.)
  • Typical Duration: 6–7 days
  • Difficulty: Moderate

Why Trek Har Ki Dun?

  • Mythical Heritage: Known as the "Valley of Gods," local legends say the Pandavas took this route to heaven. The valley is steeped in ancient stories and culture.
  • Breathtaking Scenery: Walk through dense pine forests, lush alpine meadows, and past sparkling streams. The trek offers panoramic views of Swargarohini, Bandarpoonch, Black Peak, and more.
  • Rich Culture: Encounter remote Himalayan villages like Osla and Taluka, where you’ll witness unique traditions, wooden architecture, and warm hospitality.
  • All-Season Beauty: The trek is accessible almost year-round, with snowy vistas in winter and vibrant greenery in spring and summer.
  • Wildlife & Flora: Spot Himalayan Monal, rare birds, and diverse flora. The valley is part of the Govind National Park, rich in biodiversity.

Route & Itinerary

Day 1: Dehradun to Sankri (base village, 220 km drive)
Day 2: Sankri to Taluka (drive), trek to Pauni Garaat
Day 3: Pauni Garaat to Kalkattiyadhar
Day 4: Kalkattiyadhar to Har Ki Dun Valley and back
Day 5: Return via Osla village
Day 6: Trek back to Sankri
Day 7: Sankri to Dehradun
Total trekking distance: 44–50 km, with daily walks of 6–12 km through forests, meadows, and riverside paths.

Highlights & Attractions

  • Swargarohini Peaks: The snow-capped Swargarohini massif dominates the skyline, believed to be the mythical stairway to heaven.
  • Osla Village: Visit the ancient Duryodhana temple and experience Garhwali culture in this remote settlement.
  • Maninda (Marinda) Lake: A serene high-altitude lake with stunning reflections of the surrounding peaks.
  • Alpine Meadows & Pine Forests: Walk through carpets of wildflowers, rhododendron, and coniferous woods alive with birdsong.
  • River Crossings: Trek alongside the Supin and Thamsa rivers, with their crystal-clear waters and mica-studded banks.

Best Time to Visit

  • March to June: Lush greenery, wildflowers, clear mountain views.
  • September to December: Post-monsoon clarity, golden meadows, and early snow.
  • December to April: Winter wonderland with deep snow and magical landscapes.

Who Can Trek?

  • Suitable for beginners and experienced trekkers. Children above 9 years have completed the trek comfortably.
  • Requires moderate fitness; daily walks of 6–7 hours with some steep sections, especially near the summit.

Famous Food Near Har ki Dun Trek

  • Dal Bhat: Lentils with steamed rice, a nutritious staple.
  • Roti/Chapati: Freshly baked flatbreads, often served with local vegetables.
  • Aloo ke Gutke: Spiced fried potato cubes, a Garhwali specialty.
  • Kumaoni Raita: Yogurt-based side dish, cooling and flavorful.
  • Mandua Roti: Millet flatbread, part of traditional Garhwali thali.
  • Red Rice with Local Dal: Hearty and cooked in a wood-fired oven.
  • Local Pickles: Made from regional fruits and vegetables, tangy and spicy.
  • Chai: Hot tea, a comfort in the mountains.
  • Bal Mithai & Singodi: Traditional sweets from the region.
