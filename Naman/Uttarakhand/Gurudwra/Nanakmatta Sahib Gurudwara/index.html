Key Features of Nanakmatta Sahib Gurudwara

  • 24x7 Langar: Free community meals served to all visitors at all times.
  • Accommodation: Guest rooms and dormitories available for pilgrims. Special guest house for official guests with modern facilities (prior permission required).
  • Sarovar: Large sacred water tank for ritual bathing and spiritual cleansing.
  • Prayer Hall: Spacious central hall for prayers and kirtan (devotional singing).
  • Beautiful Grounds: Well-maintained park and peaceful ambiance, ideal for meditation.
  • Historical Sites: Ancient Peepal tree (Guru Nanak Ji meditated here), Doodh Wala Kuan (well), and proximity to Nanak Sagar Dam.
  • Medical Services: Regular free health camps and medical check-ups for the community.
  • Community Services: Vocational training, food distribution, and assistance programs for the needy.
  • Accessibility: Neat, clean, and senior-citizen friendly premises.
  • Festivals: Major Sikh festivals and Gurpurabs celebrated with Akhand Path, kirtan, and langar.

Major Festivals at Nanakmatta Sahib Gurudwara

  • Guru Nanak Jayanti (Gurpurab): Celebrates the birth anniversary of Guru Nanak Dev Ji with Akhand Path, kirtan, and langar.
  • Baisakhi: Marks the Sikh New Year and the formation of Khalsa; celebrated with prayers, kirtan, and community meals.
  • Maghi Purnima: Special prayers and kirtan on the full moon day of Magh month.
  • Other Gurpurabs: Anniversaries of Sikh Gurus are observed with kirtan, ardas, and langar.

Gurudwara Nanakmatta Sahib is a revered Sikh pilgrimage site nestled in the tranquil town of Nanakmatta, Udham Singh Nagar district, Uttarakhand. This sacred gurudwara stands as a living testament to the spiritual journey and teachings of Guru Nanak Dev Ji, the first Sikh Guru, who visited this region during his first Udasi (spiritual odyssey) in the early 16th century.

The gurudwara is not just a place of worship, but a vibrant center of faith, history, and community service, drawing devotees and travelers from across India and beyond.

Historical & Spiritual Significance

Originally inhabited by Siddha Yogis and known as Gorakhmatta, the area was transformed after Guru Nanak Dev Ji’s visit. Here, Guru Nanak engaged in profound spiritual dialogues with the yogis, emphasizing that true devotion is found not in austerities or miracles, but in selfless service, truthful living, and remembrance of one God. His teachings inspired many to embrace a path of devotion and righteousness, and the site soon became a beacon of Sikh spiritual heritage.
The presence of the ancient Panja Sahib Peepal tree, under which Guru Nanak Ji meditated, and the Doodh Wala Kuan (Well of Milk) further enrich the site’s sacred aura.

Majestic Architecture & Facilities

The gurudwara complex is an architectural marvel, blending traditional Sikh design with serene natural surroundings. The main prayer hall houses the Guru Granth Sahib and hosts daily prayers and kirtans. A spacious courtyard welcomes large gatherings, while the Sarovar (holy water tank) invites pilgrims for a purifying dip, believed to cleanse both body and soul.
Additional facilities include a langar hall serving free meals to all visitors, comfortable accommodations, and a beautifully landscaped park. The nearby Nanak Sagar Dam and lush greenery make the gurudwara a peaceful retreat for spiritual seekers and nature lovers alike.

Experiences & Activities

  • Participate in daily prayers, soulful kirtans, and the tradition of Seva (selfless service).
  • Enjoy the serene beauty of the Sarovar and meditate by its banks.
  • Explore local markets for handicrafts and regional products.
  • Engage in community service by volunteering in the langar kitchen or helping maintain the premises.
  • Capture the picturesque landscapes and vibrant moments during Sikh festivals like Guru Nanak Jayanti.

Preservation & Legacy

Gurudwara Nanakmatta Sahib has undergone several thoughtful renovations, preserving its historical integrity while enhancing facilities for the growing number of devotees. The management also safeguards manuscripts, relics, and artifacts, ensuring Guru Nanak Dev Ji’s legacy continues to inspire generations.

Famous Food Near Nanakmatta Sahib Gurudwara

  • Langar Meal: Simple, nourishing, and always vegetarian. Includes roti (chapati), dal (lentil curry), sabzi (seasonal vegetables), rice, salad, and boondi raita. All are served free to everyone as part of the Sikh tradition of equality and community service.
  • Kada Prasad: A sacred sweet made from whole wheat flour, ghee, and sugar, offered to every visitor as a symbol of humility and blessing.
