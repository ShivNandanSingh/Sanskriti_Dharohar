Hemkund Sahib Gurudwara

Langar of Hemkund Sahib Gurudwara

At Hemkund Sahib Gurudwara, langar (community kitchen) serves simple vegetarian meals and hot tea to all pilgrims. Langar is available after the morning prayers, usually from 7:00 AM to 12:00 PM, and sometimes in the afternoon after the temple reopens at 2:00 PM. The meal typically includes khichdi or plain rice and tea. All are welcome, and the service is free of cost.

Hemkund Sahib Gurudwara Darbar Sahib Timings

  • Morning Darshan: 5:00 AM – 12:00 PM
  • Temple Closed: 12:00 PM – 2:00 PM
  • Evening Darshan: 2:00 PM – 8:00 PM

*Temple remains closed from 12:00 PM to 2:00 PM.

Hemkund Sahib Gurudwara Nitnem/Paath Timings

  • Morning Paath: 4:00 AM – 5:00 AM
  • Asa Di Vaar: 5:00 AM – 6:00 AM
  • Sukhmani Sahib: 6:30 AM – 7:30 AM
  • Evening Rehras Sahib: 6:00 PM – 7:00 PM
  • Kirtan Sohila: 8:00 PM (before closing)

Note: Timings may vary depending on weather and season.

Seva & Offerings at Hemkund Sahib Gurudwara

  • Selfless Service (Seva):
    Pilgrims can volunteer in the Gurudwara by helping in the langar (community kitchen), cleaning, or assisting fellow devotees. Seva is open to all, regardless of background, and is a core Sikh value practiced at Hemkund Sahib.
  • Langar:
    Free vegetarian meals and hot tea are served to all visitors daily. You may help in preparing, serving, or cleaning up after the meals.
  • Offerings:
    Devotees can make donations for the maintenance of the Gurudwara, disaster relief, or community welfare. Donation boxes and bank details are available at the site.
  • Participation in Prayers:
    Join daily kirtan (hymn singing), ardas (prayers), and meditation by the sacred lake.
  • Guided Seva:
    Volunteers may also help with organizing the yatra, assisting elderly pilgrims, and maintaining the Gurudwara premises.

    • Note: All seva is voluntary and anyone can participate. Overnight stay is not permitted at Hemkund Sahib, so all seva and offerings should be completed during the day.

    Key Features of Hemkund Sahib Gurudwara

    • Langar Hall: Free hot tea and simple meals (like khichdi) served to all pilgrims during visiting hours.
    • Prayer Hall: Spacious carpeted area for prayers and meditation.
    • Holy Lake (Sarovar): Pilgrims can take a ritual dip in the glacial lake beside the Gurudwara.
    • Separate Bathing Areas: Men bathe outside; women have a separate enclosure inside the Gurudwara.
    • Basic Rest Facilities: Resting area available at Ghangaria base camp and limited shelter at the Gurudwara (overnight stay not allowed).
    • Medical Aid: Basic first aid and emergency medical assistance available during yatra season.
    • Drinking Water: Clean drinking water is provided to pilgrims.
    • Ponies & Palkis: Pony and palki (palanquin) services are available for those unable to trek.
    • Accommodation: Accommodation options at Govind Ghat and Ghangaria (not at Hemkund Sahib itself).
    • Helicopter Service: Helicopter service between Govind Ghat and Ghangaria (seasonal, advance booking needed).
    • ATM & Parking: ATM and paid parking facilities at Govind Ghat.
    • Restrooms: Basic restroom facilities available at Ghangaria and Govind Ghat; very limited at Hemkund Sahib.

    Note: Overnight stay is not permitted at Hemkund Sahib Gurudwara. All pilgrims must return to Ghangaria or Govind Ghat before dusk.

    Major Festivals at Hemkund Sahib Gurudwara

    • Hemkund Sahib Yatra Opening Ceremony:
      The annual pilgrimage (Yatra) begins in late May each year with a grand flag-off and traditional Ardas (prayer). The first batch of pilgrims, led by the Panj Pyare (Five Beloved Ones), starts the trek from Govindghat. The opening is marked by devotional music and community celebrations.
    • Daily Kirtan and Seva:
      Throughout the pilgrimage season (May–October), daily Kirtan (devotional singing), prayers, and community Langar (free kitchen) are held, drawing thousands of devotees.
    • Special Nagar Kirtan:
      Before the Yatra starts, a Nagar Kirtan (religious procession) is organized from Govindghat to Govind Dham, and then to Hemkund Sahib, filling the route with spiritual hymns and Sikh banners.
    • Closing Ceremony:
      The Yatra season ends in early October with a solemn closing ceremony and prayers, after which the shrine is closed for winter due to heavy snowfall.

    Note: The pilgrimage season also coincides with the blooming of the Valley of Flowers nearby, making it a special time for both spiritual and natural beauty.

    Anand Karaj (Marriage Ceremony) at Hemkund Sahib Gurudwara

    Anand Karaj is the Sikh wedding ceremony, meaning "Blissful Union." The ceremony is performed in the prayer hall of the Gurudwara, in the presence of the Guru Granth Sahib.

    • The bride and groom sit before the Guru Granth Sahib, joined by family and congregation.
    • The ceremony begins with Ardaas (prayer) and hymns.
    • The four Lavan (wedding hymns) are sung. After each hymn, the couple walks clockwise around the Guru Granth Sahib, led by the groom.
    • After completing four rounds, the couple is blessed and the ceremony concludes with Anand Sahib and final Ardaas.
    • All guests are served Langar (community meal) after the ceremony.

    Anand Karaj at Hemkund Sahib is a serene and spiritual experience, following Sikh traditions and the code of conduct.

    Education at Hemkund Sahib Gurudwara

    Hemkund Sahib Gurudwara itself does not run a formal school at the high-altitude shrine, but it actively promotes education and awareness through cultural and spiritual programs for pilgrims. The Gurudwara complex houses a small museum that displays relics and historical artifacts related to Guru Gobind Singh Ji and Sikh heritage, offering visitors educational insight into Sikh history and values.

    Additionally, the Hemkunt Foundation runs educational and humanitarian projects in the region, including after-school centers for local children to learn English, Mathematics, and IT, as well as awareness programs on health, environment, and disaster relief. These efforts reflect the Sikh principles of service, learning, and community upliftment.

    Food at Hemkund Sahib Gurudwara

    At Hemkund Sahib Gurudwara, all pilgrims are served free vegetarian meals in the Langar hall. The food is simple and nourishing, typically including khichdi (rice and lentils) and hot tea. Meals are prepared and served by Sikh volunteers, and everyone is welcome to sit together and eat, regardless of background.

    There are no restaurants at Hemkund Sahib itself; the Langar is the main source of food for visitors. The Langar operates during the main visiting hours, especially after morning prayers and holy bath. The tradition reflects the Sikh values of equality, service, and community.

    Please note: Only vegetarian food is served, and the menu is basic due to the high-altitude location and limited resources.

    Hemkund Sahib Gurudwara: The Jewel of the Himalayas

    Hemkund Sahib Gurudwara, nestled at a breathtaking altitude of 4,632 meters (15,197 feet) in the Chamoli district of Uttarakhand, is one of the world’s highest and most revered Sikh pilgrimage sites. Surrounded by seven snow-capped peaks and set beside a pristine glacial lake, this sacred shrine is not only a testament to Sikh faith but also a marvel of natural beauty and spiritual tranquility.

    Spiritual Significance & History

    The word “Hemkund” is derived from “Hem” (snow) and “Kund” (bowl/lake), perfectly describing the icy lake that mirrors the Gurudwara and the surrounding peaks. According to the Bachitra Natak, Guru Gobind Singh Ji—the tenth Sikh Guru—meditated here in a previous life. Inspired by his writings, Sikh devotees rediscovered the site in the early 20th century and constructed the present Gurudwara, making it a beacon of spiritual heritage and devotion.
    The pilgrimage to Hemkund Sahib is a journey of faith, echoing the resilience and sacrifices of Sikh Gurus and their followers. The site also preserves Sikh history through a small museum displaying relics and artifacts related to Guru Gobind Singh Ji.

    Architectural Splendor

    The Gurudwara’s unique pentagonal structure, designed to resemble an inverted lotus, is crafted from white marble and concrete to withstand the harsh Himalayan climate. Its gilded dome gleams against the backdrop of rugged peaks, while the main hall offers a serene space for prayer and reflection. Doors on all five sides symbolize openness and welcome devotees from every direction and faith.
    The adjacent Hemkund Lake (Lokpal Lake) is crystal-clear and revered for its spiritual purity. Pilgrims often take a bracing dip in its icy waters, believing it cleanses the soul and brings inner peace.

    The Pilgrimage Experience

    The journey to Hemkund Sahib is both challenging and transformative. Pilgrims begin at Govindghat, trekking 13 km to Ghangaria through lush forests and cascading waterfalls. From Ghangaria, a steep 6 km stone-paved path leads to the Gurudwara. The trek is physically demanding, with unpredictable weather and thin mountain air, but the breathtaking scenery and the spirit of kar seva (selfless service) make it unforgettable.
    Helicopter services and pony rides are available for those unable to trek, but most pilgrims choose to walk, embracing the journey as an act of devotion.

    Langar & Seva

    At Hemkund Sahib, the tradition of langar (community kitchen) is upheld with humility and love. Volunteers serve simple, nourishing meals and hot tea to all visitors, regardless of background, reflecting Sikhism’s core values of equality and selfless service. The Gurudwara is maintained by the tireless efforts of devotees who participate in cleaning, cooking, and assisting fellow pilgrims.

    When to Visit & Travel Tips

    Hemkund Sahib is open from late May to early October, as heavy snowfall makes the route inaccessible for the rest of the year. Pilgrims are advised to start their return journey before 2 pm, as overnight stay at the shrine is not permitted. The nearest roadhead is Govindghat, accessible by bus or taxi from Rishikesh, Haridwar, or Dehradun. Helicopter services are available seasonally from Govindghat to Ghangaria.
    Due to the high altitude, visitors should acclimatize properly, stay hydrated, and be aware of the symptoms of altitude sickness.

    Inspiration for All

    Hemkund Sahib Gurudwara is more than a destination—it is a spiritual ascent. The shimmering lake, the tranquil prayers, and the snow-draped peaks inspire a sense of humility, unity, and peace. Whether you seek adventure, solace, or a deeper connection with faith, Hemkund Sahib offers an experience that will remain etched in your heart forever.
