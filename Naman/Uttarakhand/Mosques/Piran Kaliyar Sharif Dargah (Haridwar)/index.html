Main Offering of Piran Kaliyar Sharif Dargah

At the revered Piran Kaliyar Sharif Dargah near Roorkee, devotees offer flowers, sweets, chadar (cloth), and money at the tomb of Hazrat Alauddin Ali Ahmed Sabir. These offerings are made to seek blessings for health, happiness, and spiritual healing.
The dargah is famous for its peaceful atmosphere and is visited by people from all religions, especially during the annual Urs festival.
Offerings like chadors and sweets are available for purchase near the shrine.

Major Festivals at Piran Kaliyar Sharif Dargah

Piran Kaliyar Sharif is a revered Sufi shrine near Roorkee, Uttarakhand, dedicated to Hazrat Alauddin Ali Ahmed Sabir Kalyari (Sabir Pak). The dargah is famous for its annual Urs festival, which commemorates the saint's death anniversary and symbolizes unity and harmony among all communities.

  • Urs Festival: Celebrated for 15 days in May-June (according to the Islamic calendar), Urs attracts lakhs of devotees from across India and abroad. It marks the death anniversary of Sabir Pak and is the main festival at the dargah.
  • Highlights: The Urs features qawwali (devotional music), prayers, langar (community meals), and the famous Jhanda Mela or Flag Ceremony, where a large flag is hoisted as a symbol of blessings and good fortune.
  • Significance: The festival is known for its vibrant atmosphere, communal harmony, and spiritual fervor, with people from all religions coming together to seek blessings, healing, and fulfillment of wishes.

The Urs at Piran Kaliyar Sharif is a unique spiritual event, showcasing India's rich tradition of Sufi devotion and communal unity.

Food at Piran Kaliyar Sharif Dargah

Piran Kaliyar Sharif Dargah: Food Provided

At Piran Kaliyar Sharif Dargah, a langar (community kitchen) tradition is followed, where free food is served to all visitors regardless of religion or background. This practice was started by the revered Sufi saint Alauddin Sabir Kaliyari, who was made in-charge of the kitchen by Baba Fareed. The langar is a symbol of equality and service, and continues to this day at the shrine.
Typical food served includes simple, vegetarian meals such as roti (bread), dal (lentils), sabzi (vegetables), and rice.

Introduction

Piran Kaliyar Sharif Dargah is one of the most revered Sufi shrines in India, located near Roorkee in the Haridwar district of Uttarakhand. Dedicated to the great Sufi saint Hazrat Alauddin Ali Ahmed Sabir Kalyari, the dargah is a symbol of communal harmony, attracting devotees from all faiths and walks of life.

History & Significance

Built in the 13th century by Sultan Ibrahim Lodhi, the dargah marks the resting place of Hazrat Sabir Kaliyari, a renowned Sufi saint of the Chishti Order. The shrine is not only a spiritual center but also a testament to India's rich tradition of Sufism and unity in diversity.

Did You Know?
The dargah is believed to fulfill the wishes of all who visit with a pure heart, earning it the title "Sabir Pak" among devotees.

Architectural Features

  • Main Tomb: Enclosed by beautiful medieval jalis (latticed screens) with entrances on the east and north sides.
  • Inner Chamber: Adorned with pristine white marble and a marble enclosure around the saint’s grave.
  • Spacious Courtyard: Lined with open chambers and white marble flooring, offering a tranquil atmosphere for prayer.
  • Historic Mosque: An old mosque stands within the complex, adding to its spiritual ambiance.
  • Ancient Gular Tree: A sacred tree believed to be centuries old stands near the dargah, visited by devotees for blessings.

Festivals & Pilgrimage

The annual Urs festival (death anniversary of the saint) is a grand event, drawing lakhs of pilgrims from across India and abroad. The dargah becomes a vibrant hub of qawwali (Sufi devotional music), prayers, and communal feasts during this time.

Location & Accessibility

  • Distance: About 10 km from Roorkee, 20 km from Haridwar
  • Situated: On the banks of the Ganga Canal, surrounded by lush greenery
  • How to Reach: Easily accessible by road and rail; nearest railway station is Roorkee

Why Visit?

Whether you seek spiritual solace, wish to witness the vibrant culture of Sufism, or simply want to experience the architectural grandeur, Piran Kaliyar Sharif Dargah offers a unique and uplifting experience for every visitor.
